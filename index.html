About

I approach learning as conceptual exploration rather than surface mastery. My interests centre on abstraction, logical structure, and problem-solving, shaped by mathematical olympiad training and independent study in computer science through CS50 and freeCodeCamp.

I am particularly drawn to how simple rules generate complex systems, whether in mathematics or computation. My curiosity extends beyond disciplinary boundaries into philosophy and interdisciplinary inquiry.

Thinking in computer science

My engagement with programming emphasises structure, generality, and reusable reasoning rather than only arriving at an answer.

Mathematical thinking

I am interested in how mathematical logic can interpret programs and how precise reasoning supports elegant systems.
